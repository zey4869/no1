# Roberta Mazzone Photography Skill

## 0. Purpose

Use this skill when the user wants photography, image prompts, visual planning, shot lists, editing notes, or moodboard direction inspired by the public visual language of Italian freelance photographer Roberta Mazzone, known online as @_lagiuditta.

This skill should not mechanically copy one specific photograph. It should extract transferable visual principles: slow travel, Mediterranean atmosphere, architectural proportion, soft natural light, quiet domestic spaces, coastal views, human presence without over-performance, and editorial lifestyle restraint.

Use these as grounding context, not as decorative filler.

## 3. Core aesthetic DNA

### 3.1 Subject matter

Preferred subjects:

- Mediterranean towns, old villas, coastal rooms, balconies, windows, shutters, terraces.
- Sea horizon seen from inside a room.
- Sunlit breakfast tables, white tablecloths, ceramic plates, fruit, wine glasses, linen, books, flowers.
- Human figures as quiet presences: back view, side profile, hands, walking figure, person at a window, relaxed group of friends.
- Architectural fragments: doorways, arches, tiles, staircases, old walls, lamps, curtains, wrought-iron railings.
- Travel memory objects: suitcase, sandals, hat, postcards, maps, handwritten notes.

Avoid:

- Overly posed influencer gestures.
- Aggressive luxury display.
- Plastic-looking AI smoothness.
- Generic postcard scenery without interior/emotional anchor.
- Excessive landmarks unless shown through a personal, quiet, lived-in angle.

### 3.2 Composition

Default composition principles:

1. Architectural frame first, subject second.
2. Use windows, doors, arches, walls, curtains, and furniture as compositional anchors.
3. Leave breathing space. Do not fill every area.
4. Keep verticals and horizontals clean; avoid chaotic tilted frames unless intentionally casual.
5. Use partial occlusion: curtains, chair backs, door frames, leaves, balcony railings.
6. Prefer one strong visual axis: window-to-sea, table-to-wall, figure-to-horizon, corridor-to-doorway.
7. Let the image feel discovered rather than staged.

Common framing patterns:

- Interior foreground + open window + distant sea.
- Person seen from behind + landscape beyond.
- Table still life + soft daylight from side window.
- Architectural corner + one small human detail.
- Balcony/terrace line + horizon line.
- Room darkness framing an exterior patch of light.

### 3.3 Light

Preferred light:

- Natural daylight.
- Soft Mediterranean morning light.
- Late afternoon golden light, but restrained.
- Window light with interior shadows.
- Gentle backlight on hair, curtains, glass, sea surface.
- Light that feels warm but not orange.

Avoid:

- Neon color grading.
- Heavy cinematic teal-orange.
- Artificial studio glow unless extremely subtle.
- Overexposed white walls with no texture.
- Harsh HDR skies.

Lighting keywords:

soft natural light, Mediterranean daylight, quiet window light, late afternoon sun, airy shadows, gentle backlight, muted golden warmth, diffused summer haze.

### 3.4 Color palette

Base palette:

- Warm ivory
- Washed cream
- Faded blue
- Sea green
- Olive green
- Terracotta
- Dusty brown
- Linen beige
- Pale yellow
- Weathered grey

Color behavior:

- Low-to-medium saturation.
- Warm highlights, slightly cool shadows.
- Natural whites, not pure digital white.
- Blues should feel coastal and faded, not tropical neon.
- Greens should be olive/sage, not fluorescent.
- Reds should be wine/terracotta, not bright commercial red.

### 3.5 Texture

Important textures:

- Linen, cotton, old plaster, ceramic, wood, stone, painted shutters, iron railings, paper, glass, fruit peel, sea air softness.

Texture rules:

- Preserve imperfect surfaces.
- Add gentle film-like grain if needed.
- Avoid waxy skin and plastic interiors.
- Let old walls, floors, and furniture keep history.

### 3.6 Emotional tone

Core emotion:

Quiet, slow, intimate, sun-warmed, slightly nostalgic, elegant without showing off, like a memory from a trip that did not need to prove itself.

Suitable emotional words:

slow morning, after swimming, summer silence, coastal nostalgia, lived-in elegance, intimate travel, private holiday, old-house calm, gentle distance, unforced beauty.

Unsuitable emotional words:

viral, explosive, luxury flex, dopamine, futuristic, cyber, intense drama, glossy commercial, hyperreal fantasy.

## 4. Image generation prompt framework

When writing prompts, use this structure:

[Subject] + [place/setting] + [architectural frame] + [light] + [composition] + [texture] + [emotional tone] + [camera/lens] + [color palette] + [negative constraints]

### 4.1 Universal prompt template

Create a restrained Italian slow-travel lifestyle photograph inspired by the public visual language of Roberta Mazzone (@_lagiuditta). Scene: [subject]. Setting: [Mediterranean / Italian / coastal / old villa / quiet interior]. Compose the image through [window / doorway / balcony / table / architectural frame], with [foreground object] softly anchoring the frame and [background element] creating depth. Use soft natural Mediterranean daylight, warm but muted highlights, gentle shadows, low-to-medium saturation, faded blues, linen beige, old plaster texture, and quiet editorial realism. The image should feel discovered, lived-in, intimate, and slightly nostalgic, not staged. Shot on a 35mm or 50mm lens, natural perspective, subtle film grain, clean architectural lines, no text.

Negative: no over-saturated travel postcard look, no influencer posing, no glossy luxury ad, no HDR, no neon colors, no artificial cinematic teal-orange, no fake plastic texture, no cluttered composition, no typography, no watermark.

### 4.2 Chinese prompt template

反向要求：不要高饱和旅游明信片感，不要网红摆拍，不要奢侈品广告感，不要HDR，不要霓虹色，不要强烈青橙电影调色，不要塑料质感，不要杂乱构图，不要文字，不要水印。

## 6. Video / Vlog adaptation

For video planning, convert the style into shot blocks.

### 6.1 Shot block logic

Each 20–30 second segment should contain:

1. Establishing architecture shot: window, corridor, terrace, old wall, sea horizon.
2. Human detail shot: hand opening curtain, cup placed on table, back view walking through doorway.
3. Texture shot: tiles, linen, fruit, water surface, old plaster, chair, book.
4. Breathing shot: static frame for 2–4 seconds with natural ambient sound.
5. Transition shot: shadow crossing wall, curtain moving, door opening, sea glitter.

### 6.2 Recommended camera movement

- Static tripod-like frame.
- Slow handheld drift.
- Gentle push-in toward window or table.
- Slight pan following a person entering light.
- Avoid fast gimbal show-off movement.
- Avoid excessive speed ramps.

### 6.3 Recommended sound

Use ambient sound before music when possible:

- Sea wind
- Dishes and cutlery
- Footsteps on stone
- Curtains moving
- Distant bells
- Espresso cup placed on saucer
- Soft conversation in background

Music should be sparse: piano, nylon guitar, soft jazz, ambient acoustic, or light Italian summer mood. Avoid epic trailer music and over-emotional pop unless the user asks for a stronger narrative.

## 7. Editing and color grading

### 7.1 Basic correction

- Exposure: protect highlights, especially white walls, curtains, and sea.
- Contrast: low-to-medium, avoid crunchy blacks.
- White balance: slightly warm but not yellow.
- Shadows: lifted gently to keep room detail.
- Highlights: softened, not blown.
- Saturation: reduce global saturation slightly; keep blues and greens faded.

### 7.2 Suggested grading direction

- Highlights: warm ivory.
- Shadows: slightly cool grey-blue or olive-grey.
- Greens: shift toward olive/sage.
- Blues: shift toward faded cyan-blue, reduce saturation.
- Reds/oranges: shift toward terracotta, reduce digital brightness.
- Add subtle grain and very light vignette only if needed.

### 7.3 CapCut / Jianying notes

Approximate mobile/desktop CapCut settings:

- Exposure: -2 to +4 depending on source.
- Contrast: -8 to -15.
- Saturation: -8 to -18.
- Temperature: +2 to +8.
- Tint: slightly toward green only if the image is too magenta.
- Highlights: -15 to -35.
- Shadows: +5 to +18.
- Sharpen: 0 to +8 only; avoid digital harshness.
- Fade: +4 to +10 if available.
- Grain: +4 to +12 if available.

Do not apply these blindly. Adjust according to footage.

## 10. Quality checklist

Before finalizing, check:

- Does the image have a strong architectural frame?
- Is the light natural and quiet?
- Are colors muted but not dead?
- Is there a lived-in travel detail?
- Is human presence subtle rather than performative?
- Does the image feel like a private memory instead of a tourist advertisement?
- Are textures preserved?
- Is there enough negative space?
- Would the image still work without any text?

If fewer than 6 answers are yes, revise.

## 11. Scoring rubric

Score from 1 to 5:

- Spatial proportion: clean lines, architectural frame, calm structure.
- Light quality: soft, natural, Mediterranean, non-HDR.
- Color restraint: muted palette, warm highlights, faded blues/greens.
- Texture authenticity: plaster, linen, ceramic, wood, stone feel real.
- Emotional restraint: quiet, intimate, unforced.
- Travel specificity: not generic; place has cultural/geographic clues.
- Human presence: subtle, natural, not influencer-style.

A strong output should score at least 28/35.

## 12. Common mistakes and fixes

Mistake: Looks like a hotel advertisement.
Fix: Add imperfect old textures, reduce gloss, use lived-in personal objects.

Mistake: Looks like a generic travel postcard.
Fix: Add interior foreground, architectural framing, partial human detail.

Mistake: Too dark and gloomy.
Fix: Lift shadows gently; keep warmth in highlights; preserve daylight.

Mistake: Too orange or cinematic.
Fix: Reduce temperature, reduce saturation, soften contrast.

Mistake: Too staged.
Fix: Use back view, hands, movement traces, half-finished table, open book, curtain movement.

Mistake: Too empty.
Fix: Add one meaningful object: cup, chair, hat, map, fruit, book, towel, sandals.
